Biography

Xiang Yusheng is a Professor and PhD supervisor at Nantong University, where he leads the Intelligent Unmanned Equipment Laboratory. His research focuses on artificial intelligence and autonomous systems for real-world engineering applications.

He received his PhD from the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT) in Germany and was jointly trained at the University of California, Berkeley, under the supervision of Prof. Samuel S. Mao.

His work lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence, intelligent construction machinery, and autonomous special-purpose vehicles. His group studies how AI and robotic systems can be integrated into large-scale engineering systems and deployed in complex real-world environments.

Research Interests

  • Artificial Intelligence for Engineering Systems
  • Autonomous Vehicles and Intelligent Machinery
  • Robotics and Embodied AI
  • Multi-sensor Perception and System Integration
  • AI-driven Industrial and Infrastructure Systems

Students and Recruitment

The group recruits PhD students, master's students, and undergraduate researchers every year. Students from different academic backgrounds are welcome.

International students are strongly encouraged to apply. We welcome talented students from around the world who are interested in artificial intelligence, autonomous systems, and intelligent engineering machinery.

We particularly welcome students who are highly motivated, strong in hands-on engineering and system development, interested in interdisciplinary research, or curious about entrepreneurship.

Applicants are encouraged to send a CV, transcripts, and a short statement of research interests and long-term goals.
